## Headline
The 2021 bull lifts CAGR, but the **2022 bear sets the drawdown floor** — every
config lands at ~48-60% DD. No config reaches ≤38% DD on a window that contains
2022. 100% CAGR also out of reach over 5.5yr (that's a 2023+ 3yr phenomenon).

## Key finding: the two models want DIFFERENT regime gates
- **momentum_sp100 (top-1)**: high-beta single name — NEEDS the faster 50d gate to
  dodge crashes. fast50 → 74% CAGR / 60% DD vs 200d-only 54% / 80%. Big win.
- **n40_largecap_weekly (top-3)**: already diversified — the 200d-only gate is
  better here (58.6% / 54% vs fast50 46.5% / 52%). The faster gate just whipsaws it.
  (On the 3yr window fast50 still helps n40's DD 46→30, so the gate choice is
  window-dependent; for the long through-cycle view, top3 200d-only is the meaningful
  production config.)

Is 2021-2026 "good"? **Yes on return** (57-74% CAGR, 5.5yr; $5k → $58k-103k),
**no on drawdown** (~50-60%). If a ≤38% DD is a hard requirement you must either
(a) trade only post-2022 regimes, or (b) diversify to ~top-20 and accept ~18-20%
CAGR — there is no free lunch across the 2022 bear.
